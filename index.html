About the Event

Run With
Purpose.

Race for First Aid is a one-day track meet featuring the 100m dash, 200m dash, and the iconic 1600m. Created by Aiden Hayes as his senior project at Columbus North High School, this event brings middle and high school students together to compete, fundraise, and rally around a cause that saves lives.

Whether you're chasing a personal best or running your first mile, this event is for you. Every step, every lap, every second you run goes directly to the American Red Cross.

How It
Works

01📋
Register

Sign up online before race day. Secure your spot on the starting line. Open to middle and high school runners of all paces.

02🏟
Show Up

Come to Columbus North High School on April 4th between 10AM–2PM. Check in, warm up, and get ready to race. No experience needed.

03🏃
Race

Toe the line. Sprint the 100m or 200m, or push through the full mile. Every runner who competes makes a difference for first aid in our community.

Why We Run

"First aid isn't just a skill. It's the difference between life and loss in the minutes before emergency services arrive."

Every dollar raised goes directly to the American Red Cross, supporting their life-saving mission of disaster relief, emergency preparedness, and community health. No overhead. No admin costs. Your miles fuel real change.
